ARIES
Overview

Aries means “the ram” in Latin. It can be found in the first quadrant of the Northern hemisphere, between latitudes of +90° and -60°. Aries is part of the Zodiac chart and it was first catalogued in the 2nd century by Ptolemy.

Astrology

Aries is the first sign of the zodiac, between March 21st and April 19th. Some of the people born under this sign are courageous, optimistic, and enthusiastic. Aries do tend to be a bit moody. They do not like to be controlled and will fight back and even throw temper tantrums when they do not get their own way. Aries make good activists and are quick to jump on a new idea or for a new cause, but when that endeavor loses its excitement, the Aries person will look for new ones to pursuit..

Mythology

In Greek mythology, a golden ram was sent to save the children of a Boeotian King – their stepmother hated them and set them up as a sacrifice. When the twins real mother heard of this devious plan she sent a winged, golden ram to rescue them. As the ram was flying East to Colchis, the daughter fell off into the sea, but the son made it. In honor of his arrival the King sacrificed the ram and present Phrixus with its Golden Fleece and also his daughter’s hand in marriage.

Constellation Characteristics

Aries is a simple crooked row of stars. It's the 39th largest constellation in the night sky. This constellation only has six stars, but it is associated with several well-known meteor showers.

Most Shining Star

Out of all the stars that make up the Aries constellation, none shine brighter than Hamal or Alpha Arietis. It is the 48th brightest star and is considered a K-type orange giant. It is located only 66 light-years in distance from the Earth.
